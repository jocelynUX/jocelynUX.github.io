Regional Growth Insights and Strategic Localization Planning

Overview

I analyzed user data to uncover regional growth trends and product usage patterns from February-July 2023. While most users were in the US, we could identify clusters of growing users in other countries. Although the primary goal of reducing server costs couldn't be achieved due to third-party limitations, the insights informed future localization priorities based on regional growth trends.

Objective

To uncovered actionable insights about our user base’s growth and to forecast future trends that could guide server infrastructure decisions and localization efforts.

Tools Used

  • Tableau Desktop: For data visualization and clustering analysis.
  • Tableau Prep: For initial data cleaning and organizing usage data.

Key Responsibilities

  • Data Analysis: Analyzed regional usage data from February to July to identify growth trends.
  • Clustering Countries: Grouped countries with higher usage (such as Canada and Germany) to identify growth clusters.
  • Recommendations for Localization: Suggested regions for future localization efforts based on user activity.
  • Collaboration: Presented findings to the CEO, board member, and team.

Process

1. Challenge: Lack of Clear Goals

With no specific goals from leadership, I took the initiative to explore user data and identify regions of growth.

2. Data Collection and Preparation

  • Cleaned and organized the data.
  • Pulled user data, focusing on geographic regions and usage patterns.

3. Analysis in Tableau

  • Visualized user growth trends by country and time period (February to July).
  • Used clustering techniques to group countries with significant user bases and similar growth trajectories.

4. Insights and Findings

  • While the US had the largest user base, other countries could be clustered into similar usage groups, indicating potential for localized growth.
  • Clustering helped identify which larger regions should be prioritized once we had the funds for localization.

5. Impact

  • Strategic Localization Planning: My findings informed the founders and project manager about which regions to prioritize for future localization, supporting decision-making for when funds became available.
  • Early Patterns of Product Use: Even though the product was in its early stages, my analysis uncovered patterns of use that could guide future resource allocation and support the company's long-term growth strategy.

Outcome:

Although the project didn’t result in immediate cost savings due to third-party constraints, it provided a strategic foundation for future growth. The findings helped the leadership team prioritize countries for localization, ensuring that resources would be allocated effectively when funding allowed.
