Hidden Gems

Project Overview

Hidden Gems is a user experience design project centered around an app that helps users explore and discover lesser-known places in their neighborhood. The project aimed to create an engaging experience for users who want to uncover local gems, enjoy spontaneous adventures, and support small businesses.

Design Objective

The primary goal was to design an app that promotes serendipitous discovery of hidden gems in familiar environments. We wanted users to feel a sense of wonder and curiosity while exploring new places in their own neighborhoods, moving away from traditional research-heavy approaches to focus on spontaneous exploration.

Research and Insights

Through interviews and user research, we discovered that long-time residents of a city often become complacent in their familiar surroundings. Despite a desire for discovery, they may struggle to push their boundaries due to decision fatigue and a perception that they already know what they like. Users preferred non-intrusive, casual exploration and wanted recommendations from local residents rather than generic, broad suggestions.

Design Process

  1. Initial Ideation
  • Developed the core concept of an app that encourages users to explore hidden gems around them.
  • Created initial sketches and mid-fidelity wireframes to outline the user flow and interactions.
  1. Key Scenarios and Personas
  • Created personas like FOMO Fred and Startup Stella to represent our target users and guide the design process.
  • Developed key scenarios to explore users’ goals, motivations, and pain points.
  1. Customer Journey Mapping
  • Mapped out the user interaction with the app, including information flow and user preferences.
  • Ensured the app's recommendations were tailored to reduce decision fatigue and enhance user experience.
  1. Design Iterations
  • Gems Near You: Added category tags and icons to clarify the purpose of hidden gems and maintain spontaneity.
  • Hidden Gem Details: Improved navigation with scrolling features and integrated more detailed information on a single screen.
  • My Collections: Enhanced the nostalgia factor with stylized thumbnails and journal-like memory screens.
  • “Don’t Suggest” Option: Added specific categories for feedback to improve recommendation accuracy.
  1. Prototyping and Testing
  • Conducted user testing with a medium-fidelity prototype in Figma.
  • Users explored test locations and provided feedback, which led to further refinements.
  • Created a commercial to showcase the app’s features and user experience. Watch the commercial.
  1. Presentation
  • Presented the project to the class, highlighting key design decisions, iterations, and user feedback.

Outcome

The final app effectively combined spontaneity with tailored recommendations, encouraging users to discover new places while maintaining a connection to their familiar surroundings. The project was well-received, and the commercial effectively captured the essence of the user experience.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and Iteration: Understanding user needs and iterating based on feedback were crucial to refining the app.
  • Balancing Discovery and Usability: Successfully blending the element of surprise with useful information enhanced the overall user experience.
  • Effective Communication: Presenting the project and creating a commercial helped communicate the app’s value and functionality effectively.
